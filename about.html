Who am I?

A sophomore @ Northwestern University with a knack for aerospace and sustainable engineering. Beyond school, I am involved with materials science research, designing medical devices, and music production. Ambitions

The stars rise and set every night, constantly reminding the dreamer in all of us that there is something out there to discover. They served as shepherds for mankind, guiding explorers who yearned to understand what lies beyond since the invention of sails. Thus, humanity reached into the cosmos years ago to satiate our drive for discovery. Yet, ever since then, space has slowly seemed to drift further away, clouded by indecision and bureaucracy. In recent years, new pioneers have reignited a desire to explore the stars. Infected by this vision, I want nothing more but to be part of the next generation of explorers. Moreover, I am confident that my previous experience will allow me to hit the ground running, but I also will embrace the adaptability and unwavering perseverance at the core of engineering. After all, what better way to improve life down on Earth than by making our mark in the heavens.

I documented this intersection between spaceflight and material science in a report as an undergraduate researcher.

Art

I worked with a team to develop a waypoint-based approach towards autonomous driving. We did this by creating a convolutional neural network and simulations in Unity. We trained the NN in Unity by creating various driving simulations using various methods such as following a line of manually plotted points using PID. We then implemented the NN into Unity and were able to drive a car autonomously around a simulated Indianapolis Motor Speedway.
